Timeline

1937

Marijuana Tax Act

The Marihuana Tax Act of 1937 was a U.S. law that regulated the importation, cultivation, possession, and distribution of marijuana through taxation and strict documentation requirements. Though intended to curb recreational use, it effectively restricted industrial hemp and medical research. Violations carried fines and prison time, and the act marked a shift toward federal criminalization of marijuana.

1964

THC Isolated

In 1964, tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), the primary psychoactive component of cannabis, was isolated for the first time. This breakthrough addressed a major obstacle in medical use, as cannabis preparations had previously varied in potency due to inconsistent plant composition. Identifying THC allowed for more standardized research and dosing, laying the groundwork for modern medical cannabis studies.

1970

Controlled Substances Act

The Controlled Substances Act (CSA) of 1970 established a federal framework to regulate drugs based on their medical use, potential for abuse, and safety. Cannabis was classified as a Schedule I substance, indicating high abuse potential and no accepted medical use federally, making its manufacture, distribution, and use illegal under federal law despite some state-level legalizations. This classification has significantly influenced the legal status and research of marijuana in the U.S.

1988

CB1 Receptors discovered

Allyn Howlett discovered CB1 receptors in the brain, proving that the human body has a system designed to interact with cannabinoids. This marked the beginning of endocannabinoid system research.

1992

Anandamide Discovered

Raphael Mechoulam and colleagues discovered anandamide, a natural cannabinoid produced by the human body. It was found to bind to the same receptors as THC, helping explain cannabis's effects.

1996

California Legalizes Medical Marijuana

California passed the Compassionate Use Act, becoming the first U.S. state to legalize medical marijuana. This sparked a national and global shift in cannabis policy.

2007

GPR55

Research showed that endocannabinoids also activate GPR55 receptors, revealing new pathways for how cannabis affects the body. This expanded scientific understanding of its therapeutic potential.

2009

Stop Prosecuting Medical Marijuana Patients

Attorney General Eric Holder directed federal prosecutors to deprioritize cases against medical marijuana users in compliant states. This marked the beginning of a more tolerant federal stance.

2012

Reduce Schizophrenia symptoms

A clinical study showed that cannabidiol (CBD) could alleviate schizophrenia symptoms as effectively as a traditional antipsychotic, with fewer side effects. This highlighted CBD's potential in mental health treatment.

2016

Australia Legalizes Medical Marijuana

Australia legalized the use and cultivation of medical cannabis, joining a growing list of countries embracing cannabis-based treatments. This move helped legitimize cannabis in mainstream medicine globally.
