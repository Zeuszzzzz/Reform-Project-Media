Cartoon

The cartoon makes a powerful argument by drawing a historical parallel between the tobacco industry’s past denial of harm and the modern cannabis industry's messaging. By using a black-and-white section to depict a tobacco CEO falsely claiming cigarettes are not harmful, the cartoon invokes a well-documented moment of corporate deception that led to serious public health consequences. The color section, showing a marijuana CEO making a nearly identical claim about high-potency cannabis, serves as a stark warning: we may be repeating history.

This visual juxtaposition is insightful in that it critiques not just the cannabis industry, but also society’s tendency to downplay emerging health risks when profit and political momentum are involved. The cartoon highlights the potential danger of under-regulating an increasingly commercialized and potent product without fully understanding its long-term effects.

Professionally executed and visually strategic, the cartoon uses familiar symbolism—Congress, CEOs, and the transition from black-and-white to color—to communicate its message with clarity. It implies that just as the tobacco industry's assurances were eventually exposed as misleading, similar claims from today’s marijuana industry should be met with critical scrutiny. The cartoon ultimately calls for caution, transparency, and scientific rigor in how cannabis is promoted and regulated.
